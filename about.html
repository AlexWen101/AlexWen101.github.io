About

The 1-minute Version

I’m currently an undergraduate at the University of British Columbia majoring in both physics and math.

I have experience in nuclear, astroparticle, and high-energy physics research, and have related skills in programming, statistics, and technical problem-solving.

I plan to go to graduate school for physics or a related field and would like to eventually work in academia.

If you have a few more minutes…

I helped build and test the Geant4 physics simulations for two experiments, EMMA at TRIUMF and NEWS-G at SNOLAB. The former is a nuclear mass spectrometer designed to study products of energetic nuclear reactions. The latter is a direct-detection dark matter detector.

More recently, I worked on optimizing performance of a hybrid tree-sequence neural network used for identifying top quark decays in the ATLAS detector, which is based at CERN.

Most recently, I investigated the performance of a statistical test used for identifying CP violation in data acquired by the LHCb detector, also based at CERN.

I’m very passionate about science outreach and value the importance of good teaching.

Outside of physics, I enjoy learning about military history, military technology (especially tanks and planes), politics, and religion.

I debate competitively.

I play table tennis competitively. I often go skiing, hiking, trail running, and kayaking.
